About this app

This app predicts the early stages of Alzheimer’s disease by analyzing dementia progression.
Since Alzheimer’s is the leading cause of dementia, responsible for about 60–80% of cases, identifying dementia stages supports its early detection and monitoring.

What is Alzheimer’s Disease?

Overview

Alzheimer’s disease is a progressive brain disorder that slowly destroys memory and cognitive skills. Today, it impacts more than 55 million people worldwide.

Disease Progression

Alzheimer’s worsens over time. On average, a person with Alzheimer’s lives four to eight years after diagnosis, but some may live up to 20 years depending on other factors.

Biological Changes

In Alzheimer’s, plaques and tangles build up in the brain, disrupt nerve cell communication, and cause cell death along with most symptoms of the disease.

Diagnosis Support

Alzheimer’s has no cure and treatments are limited. Early detection is difficult, but automated systems can speed up diagnosis, lower costs, and provide more accurate insights.





Machine Learning in Medicine

Machine learning is transforming modern health care by analyzing complex data to improve diagnosis, treatment, and research.

Training

Feeding the system large amounts of labeled medical data so it can learn to recognize patterns

Validation

Testing the model in realistic clinical settings to ensure accuracy and reliability

Deployment

Applying the model in research or everyday clinical practice

Why it matters

Machine learning enables earlier and more precise detection of diseases, automates time-consuming tasks to free up clinicians, and continuously adapts to new discoveries with more data and insights.

Challenges to consider

Machine learning is not without risks. Models can learn the wrong signals, depend too heavily on specific data formats, or be difficult to integrate into real-world workflows. High-quality data and careful study design are critical to success.

The future of care

AI and machine learning are not a replacement for medical expertise, they are powerful tools that extend human capabilities. With rigorous design and thoughtful application, they hold the potential to make health care smarter, faster, and more personalized.
